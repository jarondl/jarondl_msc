Diffusion and localization in sparse networks

We consider d-dimensional networks, with dynamics described by a rate equation \[ \frac{dp_n}{dt} \ \ = \ \ \sum_m w_{nm} p_m \\ -w_{nn} \ \ =\ \ \gamma_n \ \ \equiv \ \ \sum_{m\neq n} w_{mn} \] Specifically, in what we call the random site hopping model, the sites are randomly distributed in space, and the rates depend on the distance between sites: \[ w_{nm} \ \ =\ \ w_0 \cdot \exp\left(-\frac{\left|r_n-r_m\right|}{\xi} - \varepsilon_{nm}\right)\] Where \(\xi\) is a global parameter defining the sparsity of the system, and \(\varepsilon\) is a random parameter defined per bond.

Determining the diffusion coefficient \(D\)

The long term behavior of the system is characterized by a diffusion coefficient \(D\). It is defined by the long term spreading: \[ S(t)\quad = \quad \left\langle r^2(t)\right\rangle \quad \sim \quad D t\] And it is also related to the long time survival probability: \[ \mathcal{P}(t)\quad \sim \quad \frac{1}{(Dt)^{1/2}} \] And to the low eigenvalue distribution (by Laplace transform): \[ \mathcal{N}(\lambda) \quad \sim \quad \left[ \frac{\lambda}{D}\right]^{d/2} \]

Effective range hopping

For a dense system, (large \(\xi\) compared to typical site distance), the diffusion coefficient can be estimated by a linear equation (linear in the rates): \[ D = \frac{1}{2d}\sum_r w(r) r^2 \] For a sparse system, we can estimate the coefficient by the known VRH procedure. We present the ERH procedure, which has a smooth cross-over between those two regimes. For our random hopping models it gives: \[ D_{\textrm{ERH}} \quad =\quad \textrm{EXP}_{d+2}\left(\frac{1}{s_c}\right) \mbox{e}^{-1/s_c} D_{linear} \\ s_c\quad=\quad \left(\frac{d}{\Omega_c} n_c\right)^{-1/d}\frac{\xi}{r_0} \\ \textrm{EXP}_{l}(x) \quad = \quad \sum_{k=0}^l \frac{1}{k!}x^k\]
